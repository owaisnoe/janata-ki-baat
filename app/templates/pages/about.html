WHO WE ARE — ON THE RECORD, LIKE EVERYTHING ELSE

An independent postal desk for the movement

Janata Ki Baat is a citizen-run service that converts distance into paper on a minister's desk. You pick a formal letter, we print it, post it to the Ministry of Education, photograph the envelope at the counter, and hand you the tracking number. That's the whole product.

We are not the Cockroach Janta Party. We are not any party. No political organisation gets your money, your data, or editorial control here. We built this because comment sections kept saying "we should all write letters" — and nobody was holding the stamps.

Grievances are acknowledged within 48 hours. If we ever can't run the service properly, we stop taking orders before we stop posting letters.

The money, briefly

Fees are service charges for printing and postage — itemised on the public ledger down to the paisa. Fees are not donations, and nothing is ever passed to any political party or campaign. Tips go to the Letters Fund, which sponsors postage for people who can't pay.

The line we hold

Letters are formal, lawful petitions to a ministry — the oldest civic channel there is. Our content policy keeps them that way: no threats, no abuse, no defamation, no incitement. Satire in the margins. Dead serious in the mail.
