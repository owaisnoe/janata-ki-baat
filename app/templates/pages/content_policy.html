CONTENT POLICY — VERBATIM, AS PROMISED

Letters never joke. Or threaten.

Because we are the ones physically mailing your words, we hold the pen at the last step — and we hold a hard line. Every letter we post is a formal, lawful petition to a ministry of the Government of India. That is what gets a letter received, logged and read. It is also what keeps this service, and everyone who uses it, on the right side of the law.

The fixed part

The letter templates are drafted by us: formal, factual, firm. They demand accountability; they do not abuse anyone. They are the same for everyone and cannot be edited.

Your personal paragraph — the rules

Your optional paragraph (max 600 characters) passes a keyword screen and a human read before printing. It must not contain:

  • Threats or violence — against anyone, in any language, however rhetorical.
  • Abuse or slurs — directed at any person or community.
  • Defamation — factual claims about named individuals that you cannot support.
  • Incitement — calls to unlawful action of any kind.

What happens if a paragraph breaks the rules

One of two things, at our judgement: (a) the paragraph is dropped and the template letter is posted as-is, or (b) the order is refunded in full and nothing is posted. Either way you'll see it reflected on your status page. We don't edit your words — we either post them or we don't.

Anger belongs in the mail. Menace doesn't. If you're not sure which side of the line your paragraph is on, write it angrier and cleaner — it's more effective anyway.
