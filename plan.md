## 1. Problem statement (Define)

> Young Indians who support the education-reform protests need a way to convert online outrage into a formal, physical letter to the Education Ministry, because writing, printing, and posting a letter takes real-world effort — and effort is exactly where digital solidarity dies.

**Success looks like:** a supporter goes from Instagram story → paid order in under 3 minutes; every letter physically posted within 2 working days with photo + tracking proof; a public ledger nobody can call a scam.

## 4. Positioning, tone & voice

**Positioning:** An independent citizen-run postal desk for the movement — built for everyone who **can't physically stand at Jantar Mantar** (hostel, night shift, another city, worried parents). Not a party. Not a fundraiser. A service that converts distance into paper on a minister's desk. Radically transparent: costs itemised publicly, every envelope photographed.

**The core inversion (powers all copy):** Mann Ki Baat is a monologue — one man talks, the nation listens. Janata Ki Baat is the reply — the nation writes back, and this time it's on paper: logged, tracked, impossible to un-receive. Every copy decision runs on this axis: *broadcast → post · on air → on record · listen → read · monologue → reply.*

**Register rule:** on-site copy stays **indirect** (never names the PM — "one man", "the radio show"); the sharper direct lines live on share cards and socials where the *user* chooses to post them. Satirical criticism of a public programme is protected political speech; personal defamatory claims are not — stay on the right side. And the standing rule: **microcopy can joke, letters never do.** Letters are formal, lawful petitions — that's what lands on a desk in Shastri Bhawan.

## 6. User journey (Develop)

1. **See** a share card on IG/WhatsApp → tap link.
2. **Land:** hero + live counter + how-it-works (3 steps) + tier cards + ledger strip + FAQ.
3. **Write:** pick 1 of 3 launch templates → personalize (full name, city — both printed; optional 2–3 line personal paragraph; optional postal address if they want a ministry reply). Live letter preview (the actual PDF layout).
4. **Pay:** tier + tip slider → UPI QR (+ `upi://pay` intent button on mobile) with order code pre-filled in note → user enters UTR/reference no. → "We'll confirm within a few hours."
5. **Status page** `/letter/JKB-1247` (unguessable code): Queued → Payment confirmed → Printed → **Posted (envelope photo + tracking no.)** → Delivered. Email at each hop (share moment #1 on order, #2 on proof).
6. **Share:** auto-generated card — `Letter #1,247 to the Education Ministry is in the mail.` — Web Share API to WhatsApp status/IG story; caption preset; fallback download.

**Launch templates (draft during sprint, ~350 words each, formal English):**
T1 — NEET-UG leak accountability & exam-integrity reform (demand: independent oversight, timeline for re-exam).
T2 — Solidarity with hunger-striking students & Wangchuk; humane treatment of protesters.
T3 — A parent/citizen's letter: cost of a broken exam system for ordinary families.
Each ends: signature block with the user's typed name + city ("signed via janatakibaat.in").

**Moderation gate (because WE are the mailer):** template body is fixed-safe; the personal paragraph passes a keyword auto-flag + 5-second human read during print batching. Hard rules: no threats, abuse, defamation, or incitement → paragraph dropped or order refunded. Content policy published verbatim on the site.

## 8. Architecture (Namecheap Stellar Business / cPanel — decided Jul 21)

Hosting: **Namecheap Stellar Business** (cPanel shared hosting; note it's a 30-day *money-back guarantee*, not a free trial). Replaces Vercel. What it buys us: MySQL, AutoSSL, cron jobs, and real domain mailboxes — `letters@janatakibaat.in` is a genuine trust asset. **Put Cloudflare (free) in front from day 0**: point nameservers at Cloudflare for CDN caching, DDoS shielding (matters in this climate), cookieless Web Analytics, and Turnstile — one vendor, all free.

- **Stack (Python — your home turf):** **Flask + Jinja server-rendered pages + vanilla JS**, deployed via cPanel *Setup Python App* (Passenger) · MySQL via SQLAlchemy. This replaces Next.js: on shared cPanel, Python/Passenger is far sturdier than Node, and the app is form-driven anyway (orders, admin, status pages) — SSR is its natural shape.
- **PDFs:** `reportlab` (pure Python, installs clean on shared hosting) — A4 formal letter + address labels.
- **Share cards:** `Pillow` — server-rendered 1080×1080 & 1080×1920 PNGs, cached to disk, served static.
- **Email:** cPanel SMTP from `letters@janatakibaat.in` (order received / payment confirmed / proof posted). Shared-host hourly caps (~100–200/hr typical) dwarf our 50/day. Set SPF + DKIM in cPanel and verify with mail-tester **before** launch so proof mails don't hit spam.
- **Uploads & backups:** proof photos to a non-public dir served via an authenticated size-limited route · nightly cron `mysqldump` + photo tarball, pulled off-server weekly (shared-host backups are not a promise).
- **Abuse controls:** Turnstile on order create · Flask-Limiter + Cloudflare rate rules · admin behind login (+ IP allowlist if practical).
- **cPanel cron:** expire unpaid orders (24 h) · daily-cap reset · waitlist nudge.
- **No user accounts.** Order = email (+ optional phone). Status via unguessable code link.
- **Schema:** `orders(id, public_code, name, city, email, phone?, template_id, personal_para, reply_address?, tier, amount, tip, status, utr, tracking_no, proof_url, timestamps…)` · `templates` · `ledger(type: fee|tip|postage|print|infra, amount, order_ref?, receipt_url?)` · `daily_cap(date, limit, used)` · `waitlist(email)`.
- **Payment v1 (manual UPI):** static QR of your VPA + amount + order code in note; user submits UTR; admin one-tap "match & confirm" against UPI app; unpaid orders auto-expire in 24 h. **Parallel:** file Razorpay individual KYC on Day 1; when live, swap the pay step for Standard Checkout + webhook auto-confirm (tip as line item).
- **Admin (auth-gated):** paid queue → batch "Print run" (merged PDF: letters + labels) → per-order: upload envelope photo, enter tracking no. → status flips + proof email fires → ledger entry + CSV export.
- **Tracking:** India Post has no free public API — store consignment no., deep-link to indiapost.gov.in tracking. Manual "Delivered" sweep twice a week.
- **Portability (honest note):** if Passenger misbehaves under load, this exact Flask app moves to a small VPS or PaaS in under an hour — nothing in the design is cPanel-specific.

## 9. Capacity, not virality, is the risk

- **Hard cap: 50 letters/day at launch** (one person, ~60–90 min/day). Landing shows `Today's slots: 17 left` (honest AND persuasive). Full → waitlist email capture.
- SLA promise everywhere: **"Posted within 2 working days of payment confirmation."** Auto-refund rule: not posted in 5 days → full refund, published in policy.
- Scale path: raise cap → recruit 1–2 trusted volunteers (Delhi volunteer = local ₹18 postage + same-day drops near Shastri Bhawan) → multi-operator admin roles (Phase 2).
- Float math @cap: 50×₹59 = ₹2,950/day in; ~₹2,065 postage out — self-funding day one; personal float needed only for the gap before UTR confirmation.

## 10. Ops runbook (daily, ~90 min)

1. Morning: admin → confirm overnight UTRs → "Print run" PDF → print, sign-fold, stuff, label.
2. Post office counter: book Speed Post (all to same address = fast), collect receipts with consignment numbers.
3. **Photograph every envelope** (batch shot + per-envelope) + receipt at the counter.
4. Home: bulk-upload photos, punch tracking numbers (fast per-order form), statuses flip, proof emails fire.
5. Post the daily batch photo to IG/X: `Batch #4: 43 letters to Shastri Bhawan. 📮` — the ops IS the content.
6. ePost tier: upload batch at epost-indiapost.gov.in, attach receipt screenshots.
7. Weekly: reconcile ledger, publish totals, check deliveries.

## 11. Trust, legal & safety (sober notes — not legal advice)

- **This is lawful civic petitioning** — formal letters to a ministry are a protected, conventional channel. Keep it that way: the moderation gate (§6) is the line.
- **Fees ≠ donations.** Service charges for printing/postage only; no political fundraising, nothing to CJP/any party. Say so on /about and in ToS.
- **DPDP Act hygiene:** collect the minimum; reply-address optional; purge addresses 30 days post-delivery; never sell/share data; privacy page in plain English. Public wall shows envelope photos with **sender line masked** — full photo only on the private status link.
- **Operator identity public:** your real name, photo, contact on /about. In a copycat-scam landscape, transparency is the moat — and anonymous operators are the ones who look like scams.
- **Platform-risk honesty:** CJP's X account was blocked under IT Act 69(A) (later reversed by Delhi HC); their site was DNS-blocked. Mitigations: stay squarely civic in tone, keep an exportable static mirror + repo backup, registrar lock on the domain, minimal prepaid infra (short hosting commitment + Cloudflare free; the app is portable in an hour), grievance-contact email per IT Rules.
- **Refunds:** automatic if SLA blown; refund = same UPI rail. Log in ledger.
